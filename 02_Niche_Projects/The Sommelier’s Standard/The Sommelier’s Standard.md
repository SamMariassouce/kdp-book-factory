This document outlines the professional design specifications for **"The Sommelier’s Standard."** To compete in the "Wine Log" niche on KDP, the book must move away from the "cheap notebook" look and toward a "luxury reference tool" aesthetic.

---

### 1. THE HOOK: What makes this book unique?
**The Value Proposition:** Unlike standard wine logs that offer simple "stars and notes," *The Sommelier’s Standard* utilizes a **Structured Deductive Tasting Method.** 

It is designed as a "Professional Training Companion." It appeals to the *aspirational* wine drinker—someone who doesn't just want to remember if they liked a wine, but wants to train their palate to recognize acidity levels, tannin structures, and regional typicity.

*   **The "Standard" Grid:** Every page includes a technical "Palate Profile" (a visual spider graph or scale) that mirrors professional CMS (Court of Master Sommeliers) or WSET tasting notes.
*   **The Cellar Index:** A numbered referencing system that allows the user to catalog their home cellar and find notes instantly.

---

### 2. INTERIOR ELEMENTS: The Log Page Data Fields
To maintain a high-end feel, the data fields are grouped into four logical "Phases of Tasting."

**Phase 1: The Identity (Header)**
*   **Wine Name & Producer**
*   **Vintage, Region, & Grapes (Varietal)**
*   **ABV% & Price Point**
*   **Date Tasted & Location/Occasion**

**Phase 2: The Sight (Checkboxes/Scale)**
*   **Clarity:** (Clear — Hazy)
*   **Intensity:** (Pale — Medium — Deep)
*   **Color/Hue:** (White: Straw/Gold/Amber | Rosé: Pink/Salmon/Orange | Red: Purple/Ruby/Garnet/Tawny)

**Phase 3: The Nose & Palate (The Technical Core)**
*   **Aroma Intensity:** (Low — Med — High)
*   **Aroma Profile:** (Primary: Fruit/Floral | Secondary: Oak/Yeast | Tertiary: Earth/Leather)
*   **Sweetness:** (Dry — Off-Dry — Sweet)
*   **Acidity/Tannin/Body:** (Low — Med — High 1-5 Scale)
*   **Flavor Intensity & Finish:** (Short — Medium — Long)

**Phase 4: The Conclusion (Footer)**
*   **Pairing Recommendation:** (What food would elevate this?)
*   **Aging Potential:** (Drink now vs. Hold)
*   **The "Standard" Score:** (A 1–100 or 1–5 scale)
*   **Personal Notes:** (Dot grid area for free-hand thoughts)

---

### 3. FONT & STYLE: The "High-End" Aesthetic
For KDP, black-and-white interiors must rely on typography and whitespace to convey luxury.

*   **Color Palette:** High-contrast Black and White. Use 60% Grey for secondary lines to prevent the page from looking "heavy."
*   **Primary Serif (Titles):** *Playfair Display* or *Cinzel*. These fonts evoke traditional European wine labels.
*   **Secondary Sans-Serif (Data Fields):** *Montserrat Light* or *Lato*. Clean, modern, and easy to read at small sizes (8pt - 9pt).
*   **Layout Style:** 
    *   **Margins:** Generous (at least 0.75" inside gutter) to ensure the book can be written in comfortably without the spine interfering.
    *   **Accents:** Thin "hairline" rules (0.5pt) and the use of minimalist icons (a small grape cluster, a glass, a nose) instead of bulky text headers.
    *   **Paper Recommendation:** Specify **Cream Paper** in KDP settings. It looks more expensive and is easier on the eyes than stark white.

---

### 4. MANUSCRIPT OUTLINE: Chapters & Guides
A professional interior isn't just log pages; it includes "Front Matter" that teaches the user how to think like a sommelier.

**Part I: The Foundation (Intro)**
*   **Chapter 1: The Five S’s:** A 2-page guide on See, Swirl, Sniff, Sip, and Savor.
*   **Chapter 2: The Color Wheel:** A visual reference for aging (e.g., how white wine darkens and red wine lightens over time).
*   **Chapter 3: The Vocabulary of Wine:** A glossary of "Sommelier Descriptors" (e.g., what is 'Flabby' vs. 'Bright' acidity?).

**Part II: The Log (The Core)**
*   **The Cellar Index:** (Pages 1–4) Numbered lines (1–100) where users write the wine name next to the corresponding page number for quick reference.
*   **100 Structured Tasting Pages:** The data fields defined in Section 2.

**Part III: The Reference (Back Matter)**
*   **Chapter 4: The Glassware Guide:** Which shapes for which grapes (Bordeaux vs. Burgundy stems).
*   **Chapter 5: Ideal Serving Temperatures:** A quick-glance chart for sparkling, whites, and reds.
*   **Chapter 6: Vintage Chart Basics:** A general guide on "Great Years" for major regions (Napa, Bordeaux, Tuscany).

---

### Designer's Pro-Tip for KDP:
To make this book truly "Standard" grade, use a **7 x 10 inch** trim size. It is larger than the standard 6x9, providing more room for detailed notes, and feels more like a substantial reference book in the hand. For the cover, use a **Matte Finish**—it resists fingerprints and feels more premium than Glossy.
